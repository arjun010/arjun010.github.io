Multimodal Interfaces

Abstract

Interactive devices such as tablets and touch-enabled PCs present a unique opportunity to support more expressive and fluid human-computer interaction (HCI) experiences. Leveraging advancements in hardware and input recognition technology, I have designed multimodal interfaces that allow people to be more expressive and use natural input modalities such as pen, touch, and speech for data exploration and image editing. In addition to exemplifying how multimodal interaction can support a higher degree of freedom of input, through these projects, I have also shown how we can use multimodal techniques to address fundamental HCI challenges such as discoverability of natural language input.
